SkyGeni Sales Intelligence – Win‑Rate Deep Dive (Part 2)

Win‑rate breakdown, business insights, and custom metrics

Data source: SkyGeni sales dataset (2023–2024). [file:1]

Executive summary

  • FinTech has the highest win rate at ~48%, while EdTech is lower at ~44%, showing stronger positioning in financial services than in education. [file:1]
  • Across regions, win rates are tightly clustered around 45–46%, with India and Europe slightly ahead and no geography dramatically underperforming. [file:1]
  • Additional EDA highlights three main levers: high‑intent leads, mid‑sized deals, and reduced late‑stage leakage. [file:1]

Insight 1 – Lead source quality

What we see

  • Inbound and referral leads show significantly higher win rates than outbound or cold partner‑sourced leads. [file:1]
  • High‑intent demand yields materially more wins per opportunity than low‑intent outbound. [file:1]

What to do

  • Re‑weight SDR time and marketing spend toward inbound‑like channels (content, events, advocacy, partner enablement). [file:1]
  • Tighten qualification and disqualification rules for low‑intent outbound leads. [file:1]

Insight 2 – Deal size sweet spot

Bucketing deals by ACV (Small, Mid, Upper‑Mid, Enterprise) shows win rates typically peak in the mid/upper‑mid bands. [file:1]

  • Very small deals often churn with “no decision” due to low perceived value. [file:1]
  • Very large enterprise deals stall or are lost late because of complexity and multi‑stakeholder risk. [file:1]

Actions

  • Create a fast‑track motion for small deals: simplified pricing, minimal approvals, shorter process. [file:1]
  • Build an “enterprise pursuit” motion for large deals: executive sponsor, multi‑threading, ROI/value selling, stronger stage exit criteria. [file:1]

Insight 3 – Late‑stage leakage

A disproportionate share of lost deals are lost in late stages (Proposal, Negotiation, final Closed‑Lost), not just at early qualification. [file:1]

  • This suggests issues in pricing, competitive positioning, and stakeholder alignment rather than pure top‑of‑funnel volume. [file:1]

Actions

  • Standardize late‑stage battlecards (competitors, pricing objections, ROI proof, references, procurement/legal playbook). [file:1]
  • Require structured reviews for high‑value deals before proposals go out (champion check, economic buyer, quantified value case). [file:1]

Custom metric – Pipeline Quality Index (PQI)

Definition

  • For each segment (industry, region, or rep), compute: win rate, median/mean deal amount (ACV), and share of high‑intent leads (Inbound + Referral). [file:1]
  • Normalize each component to 0–1 across segments. [file:1]
  • PQI(segment) = average of the three normalized values. [file:1]

How to use

  • High PQI: strong win rate, healthy deal sizes, and high‑intent pipeline (e.g., FinTech). [file:1]
  • Low PQI: weaker win rate and/or small, low‑intent deals (e.g., EdTech). [file:1]
  • CRO uses PQI to prioritize where to invest versus where to fix ICP, pricing, or messaging first. [file:1]

Custom metric – Late‑Stage Drop‑off Rate (LSDR)

Definition

  • Define “late stages” as all stages at/after Proposal/Negotiation. [file:1]
  • For a segment: Numerator = deals that reached a late stage and ended Lost; Denominator = all deals that reached a late stage. [file:1]
  • LSDR = Numerator ÷ Denominator. [file:1]

How to use

  • High LSDR pinpoints segments where pricing, competition, or stakeholder alignment is failing at the end of the cycle. [file:1]
  • Track LSDR by industry and lead source; target enablement and process changes where LSDR is worst. [file:1]

Recommended actions for the CRO

  • Double‑down on high‑intent lead sources (Inbound/Referral) and re‑qualify low‑intent outbound segments. [file:1]
  • Protect and scale the mid/upper‑mid ACV “sweet spot” while tailoring motions for very small and very large enterprise deals. [file:1]
  • Use PQI to prioritize investment and LSDR to monitor and fix late‑stage leakage, especially in underperforming industries like EdTech. [file:1]
